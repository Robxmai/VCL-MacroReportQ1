## Source 5: GeoScope - Trading Down Behavior
**URL**: https://geoscope-sa.com/2025/09/23/shifting-sands-insights-into-south-africas-largest-consumer-survey-trends/

### Trading Down Behavior

- **DStv subscription shift**: Premium and mid-tier packages declined 67% and 53% since 2021; entry-level packages (EasyView) surged by **139%**
- **Eating out decline**: Weekly dining occasions down **nearly 30%** compared to pre-crisis levels
- **Grocery basket shrinkage**: Nominal monthly grocery spend rose from R2,050 (2023) to over R2,100 (2024) - barely keeping up with inflation, meaning real volume declined

### Brand Loyalty Shifts
- Brand loyalty to staple categories **weakened by end of 2023**
- **2024 trend stabilised**: More consumers reported "always buying the same brands" across discretionary categories - signalling **growing price sensitivity** rather than brand exploration

### Financial Capacity Indicators
- **48% drop in saving/investing capacity** in 2024 compared to 2021
- **Top loan reason (38%)** in 2024: paying bills, food, and medical expenses (shift from education/vehicles/home improvements)

### Communal Financial Mechanisms
- Stokvels participation: **31%**
- Funeral policies: **32%**
- Receiving/sending money transfers: **33% each**

## Source 7: TransUnion Consumer Pulse Study Q4 2025
**URL**: https://newsroom.transunion.co.za/south-africans-show-determined-optimism-as-they-adapt-to-financial-pressures/

### Optimism Statistics
- **72%** of South Africans feel positive about their financial outlook for next 12 months
- 48% said household finances were better than planned in Q4 2025
- Younger generations most optimistic: Gen Z (18-28) and Millennials (29-44) lead optimism levels

### Financial Pressures
- **36%** anticipate being unable to meet at least one bill or loan payment in full
- High borrowing costs are top barrier to credit access (33%)
- Fear of rejection due to credit history affects 26%

### Credit Behavior
- **91%** view credit as key to achieving financial goals
- Only **42%** believe they have adequate access to credit
- Only **36%** plan to apply for new credit or refinance in the next year
- **44%** of those who considered applying decided against it

### Spending Strategies
- **50%** have reduced discretionary spending
- **34%** cancelled subscriptions or memberships
- **38%** plan to increase retirement savings/investment contributions
- **35%** accelerating debt repayments
- **27%** setting aside more in emergency funds or stokvels

## Key Data Gaps Identified

1. **Bulk buying vs frequent small purchases**: Limited direct data; indirect evidence suggests shrinkflation/shopping more frequently in smaller quantities

2. **Retiree spending patterns**: Not specifically covered in detail in reviewed sources

3. **Family structure impacts**: Not extensively covered

4. **Middle class contraction/expansion**: Indirect evidence only; no direct measurement

5. **Property and asset values**: Some data available but scattered across multiple sources
