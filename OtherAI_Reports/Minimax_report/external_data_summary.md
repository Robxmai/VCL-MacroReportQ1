## Quick Summary of Key External Factors

### Economic Indicators (2023-2026)

| Indicator | 2023 | 2024 | 2025 | 2026 |
|-----------|------|------|------|------|
| GDP Growth | 0.6% | 0.5% | 1.1% | 1.3% (proj) |
| Inflation (CPI) | 6.1% | 4.4% | 3.2% | 3.0% |
| Unemployment | 32.1% | 33.2% | 31.9% | 32.4% |
| Repo Rate | 8.25% | 7.75% | 6.75% | 6.75% |

### Key Findings

1. **Consumer Confidence Volatility**: Index ranged from -20 (Q1 2025 tax shock) to -7 (Q1 2026 recovery), revealing extreme consumer sensitivity to economic policy changes

2. **E-commerce Disruption**: Online retail grew 35% in 2024 to R96 billion, capturing 8% of total retail vs traditional retail growth of only 2.5%

3. **Price Sensitivity Escalation**: 51% of consumers have cut discretionary spending; DStv premium packages declined 67% while entry-level packages surged 139%

4. **Digital Acceleration**: 78% of South Africans now shop online monthly; mobile commerce dominates at 85% of transactions

5. **Essential Cost Burden**: Electricity costs up 85%, water up 68% since 2020—outpacing general inflation by significant margins

6. **Bifurcated Recovery**: High-income consumers benefitting from property appreciation (+17% in 2025) while lower-income groups remain pessimistic due to weak employment growth

## External Data Factors Contributing to Sales Decline

### 1. Macroeconomic Factors

- **GDP Growth Stagnation**: Sub-1% growth limits consumer spending capacity
- **High Unemployment (32%+)**: Creates pervasive job insecurity even for employed consumers
- **Real Income Stagnation**: Despite nominal increases, purchasing power erosion
- **Currency Volatility**: Rand weakness impacts import pricing

### 2. E-commerce Competition

- **Market Share Shift**: Online growing 3x faster than physical retail
- **Platform Dominance**: Takealot (31.9%), Amazon entry (10%+), Shein/Temu (15.3% combined)
- **Consumer Channel Migration**: 28.2% research products online before purchasing elsewhere
- **Last-Mile Logistics**: Rapid delivery services (Checkers Sixty60: 1.5M weekly orders) changing expectations

### 3. Consumer Psychology Shifts

- **Defensive Consumption**: 51% cut discretionary spending, 34% cancelled subscriptions
- **Trading Down Behavior**: Premium packages declining 67%, entry-level surging 139%
- **Price Sensitivity**: Affordability now #1 brand choice factor
- **Financial Prudence**: 35% accelerating debt repayments, 27% building emergency savings

### 4. Infrastructure Challenges

- **Loadshedding**: Affecting retail operations and shopping behavior
- **Transport Costs**: Fuel volatility impacting logistics and food prices
- **Digital Divide**: Urban (39.6%) vs Rural (24.8%) e-commerce participation gap

### 5. Regulatory & Policy Changes

- **Tax Shock (2025)**: VAT increase directly impacted household purchasing power
- **E-commerce Regulations**: New customs/VAT rules for low-value imports (late 2024)
- **Interest Rate Easing**: 150bps reduction since late 2023 providing gradual relief
