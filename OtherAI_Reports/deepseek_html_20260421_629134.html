Macroeconomic, Ecommerce, and Consumer Sentiment Overview: 2023-2026

Compiled from public sources and industry reports

Executive Summary

This report synthesizes external data on South Africa's economy, ecommerce landscape, and consumer sentiment from 2023 through early 2026. The data reveals a complex picture of modest economic recovery constrained by structural weaknesses, a rapidly growing ecommerce sector, and volatile consumer confidence. Key themes include:

  • Modest Growth Amidst Structural Challenges: GDP growth has been sub-1% on average since 2023, hampered by power outages and logistical issues. The outlook for 2026 shows slight improvement, but unemployment remains stubbornly high.
  • Inflation Easing, Rates Follow: Inflation fell from a peak of 6.9% in 2022 to a 21-year low of 3.2% in 2025, allowing the SARB to cut rates by 150bps from its peak. This offers some relief to consumers.
  • Ecommerce Boom: Online retail sales grew 29% in 2023 and 35% in 2024, significantly outpacing traditional retail. It is projected to surpass 10% of total retail in 2026.
  • Consumer Confidence Volatile: Sentiment has been on a rollercoaster, dipping to multi-year lows due to tax proposals and trade tensions, despite some tailwinds from lower inflation and interest rates.
  • Competing for the Wallet: A significant portion of discretionary spending is being diverted to online gambling, which has seen a sixfold increase in revenue in four years.

3.1 The Gambling Factor

A significant portion of discretionary spending is being diverted to online gambling.

  • Online gambling revenue increased from ~R8 B in FY20/21 to ~R49 B in FY24/25 (a sixfold increase)[reference:60].
  • It now accounts for ~66% of total gambling industry revenue[reference:61].
  • 25% of every rand spent on entertainment goes to gambling[reference:62].
  • Retailers like The Foschini Group have flagged it as a major threat, more so than Shein and Temu[reference:63].

5. Data Collection & Scraping Strategy

This report was compiled using data from public sources and reports. A comprehensive data scraping strategy would involve the following:

  • Official Sources: Stats SA, SARB (CSV/Excel downloads).
  • Ecommerce: Scraping public product data from Takealot, Shein, and others (with rate limiting).
  • Sentiment: Using APIs from Google Trends and social media (X API) for sentiment analysis.
  • News: Using GDELT or NewsAPI to track economic news and sentiment.

Ethical Considerations: All scraping would adhere to `robots.txt`, terms of service, and data privacy laws (POPIA).
